# Spiritual Guiding Principles

## 1. Intentional Discipleship Through Agency

Doctrine and Covenants 58:27–28 teaches that we should be anxiously engaged in good causes. Growth requires initiative.

In both spiritual and technical development, I must actively choose progress. My portfolio reflects that intentional effort.

---

## 2. Revelation Through Focus and Reverence

3 Nephi 13:22 teaches that if our eye is single, our whole body is full of light.

Clarity of purpose strengthens both spiritual insight and technical reasoning. When I prioritize what matters most, I work with greater discipline and peace.

---

## 3. Ethical Stewardship and Christ-Centered Leadership

Technical knowledge brings responsibility. Administrative privileges, system access, and cybersecurity tools must be used with integrity.

My professional life and spiritual commitments are intertwined. Ethical leadership is not optional—it is foundational.

# *Expanded Spiritual Reflections*
### Agency and Initiative

Doctrine and Covenants 58:26–28 emphasizes that we are agents who must be anxiously engaged in good causes. During my internship at TechCharities, I did not wait to be micromanaged. I took initiative in designing an automation tool that could efficiently collect system information and store it in a centralized database. This experience strengthened my understanding that agency is not only spiritual, but it is professional.

### Stewardship and Responsibility

Technology professionals often hold privileged access to systems and sensitive data. This responsibility mirrors the spiritual principle of stewardship. In developing my automation tool, I recognized that the system information being collected had to be handled carefully and ethically. This reinforced my commitment to integrity in both faith and career.

### Growth Through Challenges

Technical challenges during development, such as structuring the master database and ensuring accurate data collection, required patience and persistence. Spiritually, I have learned that growth comes through effort and reflection. The same principle applies in IT work: challenges refine capability.
