# Ongoing Action Plan for Spiritual and Professional Development
  # Reflection on Growth

Throughout this course, I have recognized that spiritual maturity and professional competence are not separate pursuits. My guiding principles—agency, integrity, and continuous refinement—have shaped how I approach both faith and technology.

The development of my automation tool at TechCharities strengthened my understanding of ethical responsibility in technical environments. This experience clarified my future goals.

  # Spiritual Development Goals
## 1. Strengthen Christlike Integrity in Professional Contexts

 - Conduct regular self-assessment of ethical decision-making.

 - Reflect weekly on how spiritual principles influence work habits.

 - Seek accountability through mentors and trusted peers.

## 2. Develop Patience and Humility

 - Embrace technical setbacks as opportunities for growth.

 - Seek feedback before finalizing major projects.

 - Prioritize reflection before reaction in stressful situations.

## 3. Maintain Spiritual Anchoring

 - Set structured time for scripture study and reflection.

 - Connect spiritual teachings to professional scenarios.

 - Attend the temple regularly to realign long-term perspective.

# Professional Development Goals
## 1. Strengthen Technical Expertise

 - Continue developing automation and scripting skills.

 - Improve database design and optimization knowledge.

 - Learn advanced cybersecurity principles.

## 2. Deepen Ethical Awareness in IT

 - Study real-world data breach cases.

 - Understand legal frameworks related to data privacy.

 - Advocate for transparency and responsible system administration.

## 3. Build Professional Credibility

 - Expand GitHub portfolio with refined projects.

 - Maintain LinkedIn engagement and networking.

 - Seek mentorship within the IT field.

# Long-Term Vision

My goal is to become not only a technically competent IT professional, but a trusted and ethically grounded leader. I aim to build systems that are secure, transparent, and beneficial to those they serve.
